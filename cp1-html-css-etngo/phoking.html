Now I'm sure you're wondering right about now: 'What exactly is this?' Well, allow me to introduce you to Pho King 2022, VSA UW's biennial male pageant! Let me explain a little more about Pho King, or PK for short. PK originated as a comedic male pageant but has since evolved to become a platform for identifying young males to be the changes that they want to see in their communities. The goal of PK 2022 specifically was "aMENding HIStory." Now what does that mean? To us, it meant redefining what it means to 'be a man.' We wanted to tackle toxic masculinity by facing it head-on, addressing it in the public eye, in front of our friends, family, and those closest to us. However, the first step is always to be the change you want to see. So my brothers and I had to face our own flaws rooted in toxic masculinity, acknowledge the actions and behaviors that we exhibit, whether in the past or even now, and strive to change. Oh boi, I really started blabbering right from the start. I'm sure by now you get the gist of it. I introduced it heavily but PK wasn't all super serious. We had a great time growing together, unlearning together, and relearning together. This will be my documentation of my PK experience. That's me! Hi! I know you just came from the about.html page but I'm going to reintroduce myself anyways. My name is Eugene Ngo. I'm 21 years old and a 4th year student at UW Seattle. I am half-Vietnamese half-Chinese and although it took some time, I found my community at UW at the Vietnamese Student Association. Last year I participated in VSA UW's male pageant. All of the PK boys have different reasons for wanting to do PK and I'm here to tell you why I did it, what my journey was like, and how I have changed since then! I hope you won't ge too bored and that you enjoy the ride!

Now why'd I do it? The short answer: for fun! Last year was my junior year of college. I had spent the first quarter of my college experience grinding school and for the next year and a half (all of sophomore year) we were stuck inside due to the COVID-19 pandemic. I was itching to get back and make up for lost time. I heard about Pho King through friends of mine but had no intention of doing it at first. My friends pushed me to do it and eventually I decided to give it a shot. Since there are only 12 slots available, I didn't really think I'd get it. I sent in my application and forgot about it until a few months later when I got the email telling me that I'd been chosen as one of the 12 contestants. Pretty unbelievable to me at the time. I didn't know it yet, but doing PK would lead me to create long lasting friendships and grow more than I could imagine.
